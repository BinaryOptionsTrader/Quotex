## **Basic Trading Strategies for Quotex** ‚úÖ

For beginners, starting with **basic trading strategies** can help build a solid foundation. Here are some fundamental strategies that can be applied on **Quotex**:

### **1. Trend Following Strategy**
One of the simplest yet most effective strategies is to follow the trend. If the market is trending upwards, consider placing **call options** (buying). Conversely, if the market is trending downwards, place **put options** (selling).

### **2. Reversal Strategy**
The **reversal strategy** involves identifying moments when the price is likely to reverse direction. This strategy requires keen observation of **price action** and is often used in combination with technical indicators like **RSI** (Relative Strength Index) or **MACD** (Moving Average Convergence Divergence).

### **3. Breakout Strategy**
A **breakout** occurs when the price moves beyond a support or resistance level. Traders who use this strategy will place trades once a breakout has occurred, betting that the price will continue in the breakout direction.

## **Advanced Quotex Trading Strategies** üí•

Once you‚Äôve mastered the basics, you can move on to **advanced strategies** for more experienced traders.

### **1. Martingale Strategy**
The **Martingale strategy** is a betting system that involves doubling your trade size after each loss. The goal is to recover losses with the next winning trade. However, it is risky and requires careful risk management.

### **2. Fibonacci Retracement Strategy**
This strategy uses the **Fibonacci retracement levels** to identify potential reversal points in the market. Traders look for price movements that align with these key levels to place profitable trades.

### **3. Price Action Trading**
**Price action trading** focuses on reading and interpreting price movements without relying on indicators. This strategy involves understanding candlestick patterns, support, resistance levels, and price consolidation.
